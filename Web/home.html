Milestones from sri lankan Tea Industry

It was as far back as the year 1824 in which the British brought a tea plant from China to Ceylon (as Sri Lanka was known at that point). It was established in the Royal Botanical Gardens, Peradeniya and is considered to have been the principal non-business tea plant in Sri Lanka

After almost twenty years in 1867, James Taylor, Scottish by beginning, established 19 sections of land of tea in the city of Kandy in Ceylon, at the Loolecondra Estate as the main business tea manor. The possible offer of Loolecondra teas brought about 1872, in Kandy and the primary tea transfer to London in 1873. These spearheading endeavors were finished by experimentation and further developed over the course of the years by means of the presentation and improvement of tea handling machines and techniques, by various people and organizations.

The first broking firm John Brothers and Co. was laid out in 1876 and the principal public Colombo sell off occurred in 1883 under the direction of the Ceylon Chamber of Commerce (which was laid out in 1839). The Colombo Tea Traders' Association was framed in 1894 and in 1925 the Tea Research Institute was laid out. These associations helped support the creation and product of Ceylon Teas in its beginning phases
